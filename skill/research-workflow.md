# Research Workflow

Use for full research runs.

## Step 0: Intake

Record:

- Original question
- Decision this informs
- Deadline
- Cost of being wrong
- Expected output

## Step 1: Decompose

Break the question into 5-8 sub-questions. Each should target one evidence type:

- Market data
- Protocol mechanics
- Regulatory or legal-status risk
- Competitive context
- User demand
- Technical architecture
- Counterargument
- Operational feasibility

If a sub-question still answers "it depends," split it again.

## Step 2: Assign Sources

Use at least three categories:

- Primary project/protocol docs
- Onchain or market-data dashboards
- Regulator or legal source
- Independent reporting
- User/community signal
- Internal docs or supplied files

## Step 3: Triangulate

Pick the claim most likely to flip the thesis. Check it through two different source categories and record both results.

## Step 4: Evaluate

For each finding:

- Source type: primary or secondary?
- Recency: when was it published or measured?
- Incentive: who benefits if readers believe it?
- Contradiction: what source would disagree?
- Citation check: does the source directly support the sentence?

## Step 5: Synthesize

Produce:

- Findings grid
- One-sentence thesis
- Caveats
- What would change the conclusion
- Open questions
- Source list

Stop when confidence is high enough to act for the stated decision, not when the report looks polished.
