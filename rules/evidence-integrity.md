# Evidence Integrity Rules

1. Preserve the original question before cleaning it up.
2. Do not write a thesis before decomposing the question.
3. Use at least three source categories for non-trivial research.
4. Triangulate the highest-stakes claim through two source categories.
5. Label interested-party claims.
6. Do not treat tokenized price exposure as direct ownership without proof.
7. Do not invent metrics, partners, allocations, usage, revenue, regulatory approval, or legal status.
8. Lower confidence when data definitions differ.
9. Include contradictions and gaps in the final artifact.
10. Include "what would change my mind" for every recommendation.
